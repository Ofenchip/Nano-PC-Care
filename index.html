Temperature by Season

Spring

The temperature of the PC may rise a little because the sun is out more often.

Summer

High temperatures can seriously damage your PC components.

Autumn

The temperature of the PC will decrease as the weather gets cooler.

Winter

The temperature of the PC depends on the room temperature. If the heating is on, the risks can be similar to summer.

How Poor PC Performance Affects Productivity

This ties into Human-computer interaction: responsiveness directly affects user satisfaction.

  • Slow boot times waste your time daily
    • If your PC takes 2–5 minutes to start, and you use it multiple times a day, you’re literally losing hours every month just waiting.
  • Programs take too long to open
    • Opening a browser or app should take seconds.
    • Slow systems take much longer
  • Constant freezing interrupts your thinking and this directly affects productivity.
    • You lose focus
    • You forget what you were doing
    • You get frustrated
  • Overheating causes automatic slowdowns (throttling)
    • It reduces performance automatically which means your PC protects itself, but at the cost of speed.
    • Apps become slower
    • Games lag
  • Multitasking becomes painful with poor performance so you can’t efficiently work with multiple things at once.
    • Switching tabs is slow
    • Everything feels delayed
  • Games become unplayable
    • Even simple games can feel frustrating instead of fun.
    • Low FPS (lagging)
    • Stuttering
    • Input delay
  • Video playback issues
    • Videos buffer or lag
    • Audio and video go out of sync
    • Watching YouTube or movies becomes annoying instead of relaxing.
  • Input delay (keyboard/mouse lag) which makes even basic tasks feel broken
    • You click → nothing happens instantly
    • You type → letters appear with delay
  • Fans become loud and distracting which ruins focus and comfort.
    • Fans spin faster
    • PC becomes noisy
  • Overall frustration reduces enjoyment
    • Even small delays: add mentally and no matter how much you motivated to tough it out it will impact the productivity
    • Make simple tasks feel difficult
    • You enjoy your PC less and may avoid using it.
